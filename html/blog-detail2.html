10 types of two diabetes

1. Type 2 Musings

Type 2 Diabetes health advocate Corinna Cornejo provides an unfiltered view of what’s on her mind and what she’s reading through Type 2 Musings. She started the blog several months after being diagnosed in 2009, and her perspective as a Latina with the disease based in Honolulu County, Hawaii, is reflected in the multicultural scope of her blog posts. “I go beyond my personal experiences with chronic illness to explore the impact digital health and healthcare policy are having on daily life with type 2 diabetes. My hope is that through sharing my thoughts and observations I help others make sense of health and healthcare in their own lives,” says Cornejo. Recent postings include a multicultural content calendar and her thoughts about a recent virtual summit on diabetes and COVID-19.

2. The Happy Diabetic

Affable chef Robert Lewis has been living with type 2 diabetes for more than 22 years, and his own quest to enjoy his culinary career in good health prompted him to create diabetes-friendly fare. “I get fired up to help people living with diabetes,” he explains. "It's all about turning ordinary ingredients into something extraordinary." His Happy Diabetic blog has a plethora of recipes, with nutritional information provided so you can decide whether they fit the meal plan your healthcare team has recommended. Check out his favorite recipes page, which includes instructions for Peasant Roasted Fish and pasta-less Shrimp Scampi with Tomatoes. You can also access his podcast with healthy cooking advice through the homepage.

3. A Sweet Life

Run by type 1–diagnosed husband and wife Michael Aviad and Jessica Apple, A Sweet Life strives to make living with diabetes seamless, regardless of the type of diabetes you’re managing. That means not just providing first-person accounts from other individuals with the disease, but also giving advice on relatable day-to-day questions. These days, those questions include ones about COVID-19 and diabetes. From research news and recipes to helpful diabetes management apps, this site covers every diabetes-related lifestyle topic under the sun.

4. Beyond Type 2

Beyond Type 2 is coming into its own as an offshoot of the social media–savvy Beyond Type 1 community and blog. Beyond Type 2 presents profiles and news for and about people living with type 2 diabetes that are meant to inspire and educate. For example, learn how former professional wrestler Dennis Turner (also known as Dirty Dan Denton) is “building an army” to inspire others to battle the disease, or how sports photographer Keith Crear prepares for hypoglycemic events. There’s a Spanish-language version and posts about latest medication and product news — some which involve offerings by the blog’s founding partners, among them Abbott, Lilly Diabetes, and Roche Diabetes Care.

5. DiaTribe

“Making sense of diabetes” is the tagline for diaTribe, an information hub chock-full of blogs and articles for those curious about developments in diabetes treatment, reviews of medication and products, recipes, and more. Responding to rising concerns about health equity and the impact of COVID-19, diatribe provided a recent “deeper dive” into how A1C averages vary by race and ethnicity, and they have shared advice for setting your healthy eating strategy during the pandemic. There’s a lot in this site to explore, and it’s updated frequently, so count on spending plenty of time here browsing.

6. Diabetes Strong

Ever wonder about the best workouts for improving insulin sensitivity? Or how about how to set realistic fitness goals with diabetes? That’s why Christel Oerum, a fitness guru living with type 1 diabetes made Diabetes Strong — a blog for all the gym rats out there who refuse to live in fear of rising glucose levels. Though the blog isn’t specifically written for people with type 2 diabetes, it does have relevant content, such as an explainer about how steroid medications can affect insulin resistance. Its strength is covering all topics of fitness and nutrition that anyone managing diabetes would be wise to pay attention to, like how to stick to that workout routine.

7. Diabetic Foodie

The Diabetic Foodie blog, which comes from the Diabetes Strong team, aims to show readers that “a diabetes diagnosis is not a dietary death sentence.” Founder Shelby Kinnaird, another self-proclaimed foodie and person living with type 2 diabetes, created the blog to turn that mindset around with her incredible food blog. Within, you’ll find a plethora of recipes for dinners, side dishes, sauces, desserts, drinks, breads, and more. Most of the meal recipes contain fewer than 400 calories, with less than 45 grams of carbohydrates, and focus on providing lean proteins, nonstarchy vegetables, and healthy fats. There are special sections for paleo, vegan, and gluten-free eaters. Get inspired by the blog’s stunning pictures of popular dishes, such as Low Carb General Tsao’s Chicken, and tempting sweets like the Dark Chocolate Crepes With Berry Sauce (sugar optional)

Diabetes Self-Management

Acting as a go-to source for all things diabetes, the Diabetes Self-Management blog is more than just a place for personal stories and recipes (which it also has, by the way). Stop first at the This Week in Diabetes News roundup for timely information about the latest developments relating to diabetes in the COVID-19 era. Then check out the mouth-watering recipes, such as Apricot Brisket and Spicy Pumpkin Soup. Finally, during these stressful times, peruse their advice for managing your mental wellness.

Wildly Fluctuating

Wildly Fluctuating is on a mission to raise diabetes awareness and debunk every misleading headline you’ve read online. Managed by Gretchen Becker, a Halifax, Vermont–based health writer with type 2 diabetes and the coauthor of The Four Corners Diet, Wildly Fluctuating implores others to take the chronic disease seriously by offering advice, tough love, and some consolation for the things even doctors don’t understand. In a recent post she asks, “Does Control Get Easier as We Age?” and acknowledges that anecdotal accounts must be followed up with research.

10. The Decadent Diabetic

This food-centric blog is written by a type 2 chef named Ward, who is determined to inspire others to embrace a “decadent” diabetes diet. When Ward learned he had type 2 diabetes, all those years working in family restaurants had only increased his passion to find delicious food for people living with the disease. On The Decadent Diabetic you can pore over blood sugar–friendly recipes to your heart’s content, and find meals like Ward's Cranberry-Apple-Walnut Upside-Down Cake. The homepage is busy and a challenge to navigate, but if you go to the archives page you’ll see the latest posts.
